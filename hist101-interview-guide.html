Step 1: Interview Planning

Choose someone engaged in civic life whose work relates to your democratic norm.
This should match your research paper topic
What is their civic role or engagement?
Be specific about the connection to your research

Step 2: Interview Questions

Prepare 5-8 open-ended questions. Good questions invite storytelling and reflection.
Ask open-ended questions that begin with "How," "What," or "Tell me about..."
    Connect your questions to both your norm and your historical research

    Step 3: Interview Insights

    After conducting the interview, record the key insights and quotations.
    Focus on insights relevant to your democratic norm
    Choose quotes that could strengthen your paper's argument

    Step 4: Connecting to History

    Link the interview to your Revolutionary-era analysis. This is where past and present come together.
    Look for recurring themes across time periods
    Connect to the idea that the Revolution's promises are still being negotiated

    Step 5: Paper Integration

    Plan how you'll use the interview in your historical analysis.
    Think about specific paragraphs or sections of your paper
    The interview should deepen your historical analysis, not just add modern context
