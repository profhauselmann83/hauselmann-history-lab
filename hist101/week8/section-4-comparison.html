Section 4: Document Comparison & Synthesis

Bringing It All Together

Comparing Three Documents

You've now examined three critical documents:

  1. The Declaration of Independence — Listed grievances about concentrated power
  2. The Articles of Confederation — Created a weak national government
  3. State Constitutions — Created frameworks for state governments

Now let's compare them to see what Americans actually feared and what they tried to create.

Deep Comparison Questions

The Declaration complained about a king with unlimited power. But what did the Articles of Confederation and state constitutions actually say about executive power? What patterns do you notice? Why do you think the Founders made these choices?
Grievance 17 said: "For imposing taxes on us without our consent." This was a major complaint. How did the Articles of Confederation and state constitutions handle taxation? Who had the power to tax? Who had to agree?
The Founders were terrified of concentrated power. But governments need some power to function. Based on what you've learned about the Articles and state constitutions, were the Founders more afraid of too much government power, or too little? What evidence supports your answer?

Final Synthesis: Revisiting the Historians

Question 4: Which historian was right?

At the beginning, you encountered two historians debating why the Founders wrote the Declaration:

  • Bailyn's interpretation: The Declaration was driven by ideology—belief in liberty and fear of tyranny
  • Zinn's interpretation: The Declaration was designed to unite different classes against England by using inspiring language

Now that you've seen what kind of governments the Founders actually created, which historian seems more right to you? What does their choice to create deliberately weak governments tell us about their real motivations?

You can change your mind from your earlier answer—that's the whole point of this kind of historical thinking.
