üéôÔ∏è Part 1: Recording Your Interview

You have several good options for recording a 15‚Äì20 minute audio interview. Choose the method that works best for your situation.

Recording Tools

üì± iPhone Voice Memos Easy

Built-in app on any iPhone. Open Voice Memos, tap the red button to record. Saves as M4A ‚Äî ready to upload.

üì± Android Recorder Easy

Look for Voice Recorder or Sound Recorder in your apps. Most save as MP3 or M4A automatically.

ü¶¶ Otter.ai Recommended

Records audio and automatically generates a transcript. Free account available. Works in person or on a call.

üíª Zoom

If conducting your interview via video call, use Zoom's built-in record feature. Export the audio-only MP3 from your recordings folder.

üí° Tip: If your interview is in person, place your phone face-down on a flat surface between you and your interviewee for the clearest audio. Test a 30-second recording first to check the volume.

Using Otter.ai (Step-by-Step)

ü¶¶ Getting Started with Otter.ai

  1. Go to otter.ai and create a free account using your email.
  2. On the dashboard, tap "Record" (microphone icon) to start a new recording.
  3. Otter will transcribe your interview in real time as you speak.
  4. When finished, tap Stop. Your transcript will appear automatically within a few minutes.
  5. To export: click the three-dot menu ‚Üí Export ‚Üí choose TXT or DOCX for the transcript, and MP3 for the audio.
  6. Review and lightly clean up the transcript before submitting (see Part 3 below).
üí° Tip: Otter's auto-transcription is not perfect ‚Äî it may mishear names or stumble on unusual words. Always proofread before submitting.

üìÑ Part 3: Preparing Your Transcript

Your transcript should be a typed, word-for-word record of the interview. Light paraphrasing for clarity is acceptable. Submit as a Word (.docx) or PDF file.

Formatting Requirements

  • Use your first name only for yourself and the interviewee's first name only (no last names, no identifying information)
  • Label each speaker clearly at the start of each turn
  • Include the date, location, and approximate duration at the top
  • Use standard formatting: 12pt font, double-spaced, 1-inch margins
  • Minimum length: typically 3‚Äì5 pages for a 15‚Äì20 minute interview

Sample Transcript Format

Interview Transcript Date: March 15, 2026 Location: In person (Modesto, CA) Duration: Approximately 18 minutes Interviewer: [Your first name] Interviewee: Maria --- [Your name]: I want to start with a broad question. When you hear the word "liberty," what does that mean to you? Maria: That's a big one. I think of it as being able to make my own choices ‚Äî about my life, my family, my work ‚Äî without the government or anyone else telling me what I have to do. But I also think there are limits. Your freedom ends where mine begins, right? [Your name]: Do you think Americans today value liberty the same way the founders did? Maria: Honestly, I don't think most people think about the founders at all. We think about our own lives. But I think the idea is still there ‚Äî this sense that America is supposed to be a place where you can start over, make something of yourself. Whether that's actually true for everyone is a different question. --- [Continue for full interview...]
üí° Using Otter.ai? Export the transcript as a DOCX, then reformat it to match the sample above. Otter formats speaker labels differently ‚Äî adjust them before submitting.
‚ö†Ô∏è Privacy reminder: Do not include your interviewee's last name, address, workplace, or any other identifying information in your transcript.

üì§ Part 4: Submitting in Canvas

You will submit two separate files to the Canvas assignment: your audio recording and your transcript.

  1. Log in to Canvas and navigate to HIST 101 ‚Üí Assignments.
  2. Click on "Interview: Audio Recording & Transcript".
  3. Click "Submit Assignment" in the upper right.
  4. Under File Upload, click "Choose File" and select your audio file (MP3, M4A, or WAV).
  5. Click "Add Another File" and upload your transcript (DOCX or PDF).
  6. Add a brief comment noting your interviewee's first name and the date of the interview.
  7. Click "Submit Assignment". You will receive a confirmation email.
üí° Tip: Submit at least a few hours before the deadline in case you run into technical issues. Canvas sometimes takes a few minutes to process large audio files.

‚úÖ Submission Checklist

Before you hit submit, confirm you have everything:

  • Audio file is MP3, M4A, or WAV (not a video file)
  • Audio is 15‚Äì20 minutes in length
  • Transcript includes date, location, and duration at the top
  • Transcript uses first names only ‚Äî no identifying information
  • Transcript is formatted as DOCX or PDF
  • Both files uploaded to the correct Canvas assignment
  • You've received a Canvas submission confirmation
