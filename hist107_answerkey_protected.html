PART 1 Historical Thinking Skills & Core Frameworks
Sourcing
Who created it? When? Why? Consider the author's purpose and perspective.
Contextualization
What else was happening at the time? What broader forces shaped this source?
Corroboration
How do multiple sources compare? Why do they differ? What can we infer from agreement or disagreement?
Interpretation
What most likely happened based on evidence? What argument does the evidence support?
Q2 ‚Äî Beckert passage: which skill explains why power is described as "limited"?
Sourcing ‚Äî understanding Beckert's purpose: he describes European power as "limited" to show that participation ‚â† control. Europeans were present in Indian markets but depended entirely on banias for credit and access.
Participation ‚â† Control
Being present in a system doesn't mean dominating it or setting its rules.
Example: European merchants in Indian cotton markets (1600s‚Äì1700s) participated but relied on banias for credit, coordination, and access to weavers. They couldn't control prices or production.
Capacity ‚â† Control
Having the technology or resources to act does not guarantee global dominance.
Example: Zheng He commanded superior ships and navigational knowledge, yet the Ming Dynasty did not establish an overseas empire ‚Äî illustrating that capability alone doesn't create sustained global control.
Multipolar World
Multiple centres of economic and political power existing simultaneously ‚Äî no single civilisation dominated.
3 centres before 1500: Ottoman Empire, Mughal India, Ming China (also accept: Safavid Empire, East African city-states, Southeast Asian states)
War Capitalism (Beckert)
The fusion of merchant capital, state violence, and overseas empire to reorganise global production.
It involves: armed trade, coercion, and legal asymmetry (monopoly charters that gave companies special rights unavailable to local competitors)

PART 3 Important Arguments
Beckert's Main Argument about Cotton
a. Cotton was global before Europe mattered.
b. European "breakthrough" = violence + state backing ‚Äî NOT just technology.
c. War capitalism came before industrial capitalism.
Pomeranz on the Great Divergence
Europe and Asian regions were comparable until ~1700.
Europe escaped ecological limits through:
  i. Coal (geography ‚Äî coal deposits near population centres)
  ii. Colonies / Ghost Acres (American land + forced labour)
Ferguson's "Competition" Argument
Competition explains state capacity.
BUT: doesn't explain where surplus came from.
Missing: coercion, slavery, colonial extraction.

Q19 ‚Äî Contingency means
Historical outcomes were not inevitable; they depended on specific conditions, choices, and accidents. Things could have turned out differently.
