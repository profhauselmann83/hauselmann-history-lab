RLB - Reichslandbund

National Agrarian League

National Conservative / Authoritarian / Agrarian

At a Glance

  • Type: Agrarian Indeterminate (special interest group)
  • Founded: Long-established voice of German agriculture
  • Reichstag Seats: Part of 135 Indeterminate mandates
  • Support Base: Junker elite (Prussian landowning aristocracy)
  • Stance on Republic: Opposed - like DNVP, seeks monarchy/authoritarianism
  • Key Alliance: Acts as agrarian wing of DNVP; member of Green Front

Who We Are

The small but influential RLB has long been the dominant voice of German agriculture. Although we continue to claim to speak for all German farmers, in practice we are an increasingly isolated venue for the Junker elite.

The Junker Class

We represent the Prussian landowning aristocracy - the Junkers:

Who we are:

  • East Elbian aristocrats: Large estates east of the Elbe River (East Prussia, Pomerania, Brandenburg)
  • Protestant nobility: Lutheran faith, conservative values
  • Military tradition: Officer corps dominated by Junker families
  • Social elite: Intermarried with royalty and high nobility
  • Political dominance (pre-1918): Controlled Prussian state, German military, civil service

Our estates:

  • Thousands of acres of grain fields
  • Employ hundreds of agricultural workers
  • Produce for export and urban markets
  • Capital-intensive, mechanized agriculture
  • Deeply in debt (but politically powerful)

Famous Junker: President Paul von Hindenburg - epitome of Junker values

⚠️ The Republic Threatens Everything We Are

The November Revolution (1918) destroyed our world:

What we lost:

  • Kaiser Wilhelm II: Abdicated, fled to Netherlands
  • Monarchy: Replaced by democratic Republic
  • Political dominance: Universal suffrage ended Prussian three-class voting system that gave us control
  • Military autonomy: Treaty of Versailles limited Reichswehr to 100,000
  • East Prussia: Cut off by Polish Corridor
  • Respect and deference: Socialists and democrats mock aristocracy

What threatens us now:

  • Land reform: SPD and KPD want to confiscate our estates
  • Cheap imports: American grain undercuts our prices
  • Democracy: Numbers favor workers, not aristocrats
  • Bolshevism: Marxists openly call for expropriation
  • Loss of subsidies: State support threatened by fiscal crisis

Our Policies Remain Tied to the Kaiserreich

In most regards, our policies remain firmly tied to the agrarian goals of the Kaiserreich:

  • High grain tariffs: Protect estates from foreign competition
  • Osthilfe (Eastern Aid): State subsidies for East Elbian agriculture
  • No land reform: Estates are private property, sacred and inviolable
  • Cheap labor: Keep agricultural workers' wages low
  • Authoritarian government: Restore monarchy or establish dictatorship
  • Military autonomy: Reichswehr independent of civilian control

Core Beliefs

Aristocratic Order and Hierarchy

We believe in natural aristocracy - society organized by birth and rank:

  • Social hierarchy is natural: Some are born to lead, others to follow
  • Nobility has duties: Noblesse oblige - we lead, protect, and provide
  • Democracy is mob rule: Masses lack wisdom to govern
  • Tradition and order: Centuries of Junker leadership served Prussia well
  • Land and blood: Connection to soil through generations creates legitimacy

Restoration of Monarchy

The Republic is illegitimate - born of betrayal and defeat:

  • November criminals: Socialists stabbed Germany in the back
  • Kaisertreue (loyalty to Kaiser): We swore oaths to Wilhelm II
  • Restore Hohenzollerns: Or at least establish constitutional monarchy
  • King and country: Monarchy embodies nation's soul
  • Parliamentary democracy is chaos: Multiple parties create instability

Protection of Junker Estates

Our estates are more than property - they are our heritage and Germany's strength:

Why estates matter:

  • Food production: Feed the nation
  • Employment: Provide work for agricultural laborers
  • Social stability: Paternalistic care for workers
  • Military leadership: Officers come from estate-owning families
  • Cultural preservation: Maintain traditional German values

What we demand:

  • High grain tariffs: Minimum 35-40 marks per ton to block cheap imports
  • Osthilfe subsidies: Direct state aid for indebted estates
  • No land reform: Confiscation is theft and Bolshevism
  • Cheap credit: State-backed loans at low interest
  • Labor discipline: Prevent unionization of farm workers

Opposition to "Agro-Bolshevism"

We fight against ANY form of agricultural collectivization or land reform:

What we oppose:

  • SPD land reform proposals: "Socialization" of unproductive estates
  • KPD expropriation: Confiscation without compensation
  • Settlement Law (1919): Authorized redistribution (never seriously implemented, we blocked it)
  • Small farmer demands: Breaking up estates to create family farms
  • ANY criticism of Junkers: We are Germany's backbone

Our counterargument:

  • Large estates are EFFICIENT (mechanized, scientific)
  • Small farms are INEFFICIENT (subsistence, backward)
  • Confiscation violates property rights
  • Land reform = Bolshevism = road to Soviet-style collectivization
  • Germany needs strong estates, not peasant plots

Nationalism and Revanchism

We are ultra-nationalist:

  • Versailles is criminal: Lost territories must be recovered
  • Polish Corridor: Intolerable wound separating East Prussia from Reich
  • Lebensraum in the East: Germany needs expansion into Poland, Ukraine
  • Stab-in-the-back myth: Jews and Marxists caused defeat, not military failure
  • Rearmament essential: Rebuild military to restore German power

Authoritarian Government

Democracy must be replaced or bypassed:

  • Presidential government: Use Hindenburg's Article 48 powers
  • Rule by decree: Bypass Reichstag entirely
  • Ban Marxist parties: SPD and KPD are enemies of state
  • Corporatist chamber: Replace Reichstag with estates/professions representation
  • Military dictatorship: Acceptable if restores order and protects estates

The Junker Crisis

⚠️ Estates Drowning in Debt

Despite political power, Junker estates are in economic crisis:

The problems:

  • Postwar grain price collapse: Prices fell after WWI ended
  • Failure to modernize: Many estates still use outdated methods
  • East Prussia cut off: Polish Corridor disrupted markets
  • Cheap imports: American, Argentine grain floods market
  • High debt loads: Borrowed during good years, can't repay now
  • Labor costs rising: Workers demand higher wages
  • Lost wartime profits: WWI created demand; peacetime doesn't

Result: Without high tariffs and Osthilfe subsidies, many estates would go bankrupt.

The Osthilfe Scandal

State Subsidies for Indebted Estates

Osthilfe (Eastern Aid): Government program providing subsidies to East Elbian agriculture

Official justification:

  • Support struggling farmers in border regions
  • Maintain food production
  • Prevent depopulation of East Prussia
  • National security (border defense)

Reality:

  • Money goes overwhelmingly to LARGE estates, not small farmers
  • Junkers use subsidies to pay debts and maintain lifestyle
  • Little productive investment
  • Inefficient estates propped up artificially
  • Costs taxpayers millions while unemployment soars

Growing scandal: Urban voters and small farmers increasingly angry that state subsidizes aristocratic estates during economic crisis while cutting unemployment benefits.

The Grain Tariff Debate

RLB Position (High Tariffs)

Demand: Tariffs of 35-40 marks per ton on imported grain

Beneficiaries: Large estates producing for market

Argument: Autarky (self-sufficiency); national security; protect German agriculture

Opposition (Lower Tariffs)

Position: Lower tariffs to 10-15 marks per ton

Beneficiaries: Urban workers (cheaper food), small farmers (lower costs)

Argument: High tariffs help Junkers, hurt everyone else; workers can't afford expensive bread

The contradiction: High tariffs help large estates but:

  • Raise food prices for workers (political backlash)
  • Don't help small farmers much (they don't produce for export)
  • Actually HURT small farmers (raise their costs for feed, equipment)
  • Economically inefficient (protect unproductive estates)

But we demand high tariffs anyway because they protect OUR estates.
