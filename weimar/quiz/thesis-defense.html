📋 How to Write Your Thesis Defense

What this paper is

A one-page argument defending one of the four thesis options from your role sheet. Your evidence must come from the primary sources on the course website. You will present and defend this argument in Session 3 — be ready for cross-examination from other characters.

Suggested structure (1 page ≈ 250–400 words)
  • Opening sentence: State your thesis directly and identify your character.
  • Evidence 1: Name a specific primary source from the course website, quote or paraphrase a key passage, and explain how it supports your thesis.
  • Evidence 2: Repeat with a second primary source, preferably from a different faction or document type.
  • Character reflection: Does your character naturally hold this view? If yes, why? If no, where do you diverge and why?
  • Conclusion: One to two sentences restating your argument and its significance.
