Project Overview

For this research project, you will be working in groups of 3‚Äì4 students. Each group will study one shared commodity, but each member will be responsible for a distinct analytical lens. Your individual work feeds into a shared archive, concept map, and poster.

Key Principle: This structure is designed to support collaboration without losing individual accountability. You are building one historical investigation together, not four separate mini-projects.

How Group Work Will Function

The Big Idea

Your group is building one historical investigation together. You are responsible for:

  • Talking through sources ‚Äì sharing what you've found and why it matters
  • Debating interpretations ‚Äì challenging each other's assumptions
  • Revising your ideas based on feedback ‚Äì accepting that research is iterative
Important: At the same time, each student has a clearly defined role and individual responsibilities. You are not just dividing labor‚Äîyou are building historical knowledge together.

Project Components

GROUP INDIVIDUAL Component 1: Curated Mini-Archive

Group Requirement

Your group will submit one combined archive containing 12‚Äì16 total sources that meet the original project criteria.

Individual Responsibility

Each student must contribute:

  • 3‚Äì4 sources connected to their assigned lens
  • Full annotations for their sources

For each source, you must include:

  • Full citation (Turabian style)
  • Source type
  • Who produced it and when
  • What historical question it helps answer
  • What it cannot tell us
  • One question the source raises
‚ö†Ô∏è Grading Note: You are graded on quality of selection and explanation, not just finding sources.

GROUP INDIVIDUAL Component 2: Archive Rationale

Group Portion

Your group will submit one shared Archive Rationale (750‚Äì1000 words) that explains:

  • Why these sources were chosen
  • Major themes and patterns across the archive
  • Power, perspective, and missing voices
  • Connections to capitalism, empire, and labor
  • Comparison to cotton using Beckert

Individual Reflection Paragraph

Each student must also write a short reflection paragraph (150‚Äì250 words) addressing:

  • How your lens shaped the archive
  • What your sources reveal that others do not
  • What limits or silences remain from your perspective
Format Note: These paragraphs may be included as labeled subsections at the end of the group rationale.

GROUP Component 3: Concept Map or Global Flow Diagram

Your group will create one shared visual map showing:

  • Production regions
  • Labor systems
  • Trade routes
  • Imperial or corporate power
  • Sites of coercion or extraction
Integration Requirement: This should reflect all lenses, not just one.

GROUP Component 4: Visual Research Poster

Your group will present one conference-style research poster that integrates all lenses.

The poster must include:

  • Commodity overview
  • Pre-imperial context
  • Empire & coercion
  • Global capitalism
  • Comparison to cotton
  • Consequences today
  • Central claim (2‚Äì3 sentences)
  • Selected sources
Presentation Expectation: All group members should be prepared to explain any section of the poster.
