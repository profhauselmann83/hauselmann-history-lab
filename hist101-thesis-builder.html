  • Connect to consequences by explaining what the Revolution's approach reveals

Example Thesis Statements

Example 1 (Civic Participation):

"During the American Revolution, debates over civic participation reveal that democratic freedom depended on selective inclusion, as revolutionary leaders championed popular sovereignty through town meetings and petitions while simultaneously excluding women, enslaved people, and Indigenous nations from political rights, establishing a pattern of contested citizenship that defined American democracy's limits."

Example 2 (Compromise):

"Although revolutionary leaders claimed to value compromise as essential to republican government, the practice of this norm during the Constitutional Convention shows that political compromise often preserved elite power and perpetuated slavery, demonstrating how democratic ideals could be invoked to justify profoundly undemocratic outcomes."

Example 3 (Respect for Truth):

"The consequences of the American Revolution demonstrate that respect for truth was both a revolutionary weapon and a tool of exclusion, as Patriots used factual evidence and reasoned argument to challenge British authority while simultaneously suppressing Loyalist perspectives and denying the truth of Indigenous land rights."
