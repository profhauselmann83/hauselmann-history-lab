Overview

This assignment invites you to reflect on the ideas presented by Trevor Jackson in his lecture "Capitalism or Freedom?" You will explore the history of how capitalism developed as an economic system, how property rights were created and defended, and what it means to think about freedom within a capitalist economy. By examining the historical origins of capitalism, you'll develop a more critical understanding of how economic systems shape the choices available to us—and to whom.

Student Information

Choose Your Prompt

Prompt 1: Property and Power

Trevor Jackson traced how property rights were created and defended through law and sometimes violence. He showed that what makes something "yours" is ultimately the state's willingness to enforce your claims against others. Reflect on this:

  • How does understanding property as something that had to be created (rather than naturally existing) change how you think about it?
  • Jackson discussed how colonists claimed Native American land by saying it was "untransformed" and therefore unowned. What's the danger in this logic?
  • How does the history of property creation connect to who has freedom and who doesn't today?

Prompt 2: Labor, Freedom, and Choice

Jackson explored how "free labor" (the ability to quit your job) is actually a recent invention, and how the Industrial Revolution changed what freedom at work means. He showed the story of hand-loom weavers who were displaced by machines. Reflect on this:

  • What does "free labor" actually mean? Does working for a wage feel like freedom, or does it involve different kinds of constraints?
  • Jackson suggested we might be living through a modern version of the weavers' story with AI and automation. What parallels do you see?
  • How might the state protect workers being displaced by technology, and what would that require?

Prompt 3: Capitalism as Promise vs. Reality

Jackson discussed how early advocates for capitalism (like Adam Smith) hoped markets would make us better people and foster moral community. But he also showed how capitalism actually produces unequal distributions of freedom and unfreedom. Reflect on this:

  • What was the original hope for capitalism? Why do you think it hasn't worked out that way?
  • Jackson said capitalism produces both freedom and unfreedom. Who gets freedom in a capitalist economy, and who doesn't?
  • How does understanding capitalism's history help us imagine different economic systems?
