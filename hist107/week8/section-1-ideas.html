Section 1: Ideas Shape the World

From thought → claim → movement → institution

Opening Frame

In the late 18th and 19th centuries, revolutions weren’t only about bread, taxes, or kings. They were also battles over meaning: What is a “right”? Who counts as “the people”? Who gets included when someone says “all men are born free and equal”?

Your job in this activity is to track the chain: idea → action → consequence.

Working definition: An idea is “any conception existing in the mind as a result of mental understanding, awareness, or activity.”

That sounds abstract — so the real question is: How does an idea become real power?
