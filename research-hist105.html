📋 Research Paper Assignment

Topic: Analyze the Weimar Republic's collapse and compare it to one authoritarian government of your choice (1920–present)

Length: 6–8 pages (1,500–2,000 words)

Components:

  • Deep historical analysis of the Weimar Republic using course primary sources and Pelz
  • Independent research on a second authoritarian government (your choice from the approved list)
  • Comparative analysis identifying patterns of democratic failure across both cases
  • Minimum 5 scholarly sources + 2 primary sources + Pelz, A People's History of Modern Europe

Citation Style: Chicago/Turabian or MLA

Step-by-Step Check-In Assignments

Research Check-Ins

📚 Check-In #1: Research Materials List & Summaries

Identify and annotate your sources for both case studies: at least 2 scholarly sources each for Weimar and your chosen government, plus at least 2 primary sources (at least 1 from the Weimar period). Write a 3–5 sentence summary for each source explaining its argument, how you'll use it, and any perspective or bias to note.

Best for: Getting started — gathering high-quality sources before you begin writing

Open Check-In #1 →

✏️ Check-In #2: Thesis Statement & Research Question

Draft a 1–2 sentence thesis arguing why the Weimar Republic collapsed and what your comparative case reveals. Add a 1-paragraph explanation of your argument and develop a guiding research question that frames your paper.

Best for: Sharpening your central argument and comparative focus before drafting

Open Check-In #2 →

🗂️ Check-In #3: Paper Outline

Build a structured outline covering your introduction, Weimar section, chosen government section, comparative analysis, and conclusion. For each section, identify specific primary sources and key evidence you'll use. Pinpoint where your argument is strong and where you need more research.

Best for: Structuring your argument logically and mapping your evidence before writing

Open Check-In #3 →

🪝 Check-In #4: Introductory Paragraph

Draft your opening paragraph with a compelling hook rooted in the Weimar period or your chosen government, brief context for both case studies, a clear thesis statement, and a roadmap previewing your major points.

Best for: Starting strong — your intro sets the analytical tone for the whole paper
