About This Documentary

“Star Wars” (1981–1988)

This is Episode 22 of CNN's landmark 24-part series The Cold War, narrated by Kenneth Branagh. It covers Ronald Reagan's first term as president, from his tough anti-Soviet rhetoric and the Strategic Defense Initiative (SDI), through the near-catastrophic escalation of tensions in the early 1980s, to his eventual engagement with Soviet leader Mikhail Gorbachev and the beginning of arms reduction negotiations.

As you watch, pay attention to three key tensions: (1) Reagan's moral opposition to nuclear deterrence versus the military logic of MAD (Mutually Assured Destruction); (2) the fear and miscalculation on both sides—the KGB's genuine belief that the West was planning an attack, the American confidence in technological superiority; (3) how Reagan's personal diplomacy with Gorbachev began to shift the Cold War trajectory. The episode asks: What role did individual leaders, rhetoric, and ideology play in bringing the world to the brink of nuclear war—and then pulling back?
