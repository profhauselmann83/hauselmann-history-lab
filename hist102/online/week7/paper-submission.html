Turabian Parenthetical (Author-Date) Citation Guide

Place citations directly after the information or quotation they support, before the period.

Basic format — one author
(Author's Last Name Year, page number)
(Haass 2023, 47)
Direct quotation
"Norms are the unwritten rules that fill the vast space between what the law requires and what human behavior is capable of" (Haass 2023, 112).

Place the citation after the closing quotation mark, before the period.

Paraphrase or summary
The collapse of Reconstruction norms demonstrates that legal rights without cultural enforcement are hollow (OpenStax, Ch. 16.3).

No quotation marks needed when paraphrasing — but you must still cite the source.

Citing Haass by chapter
(Haass 2023, chap. 6)
Citing OpenStax
(OpenStax, Ch. 19.2)

OpenStax has no page numbers — cite by chapter and section number.

Citing a primary source
(Plessy v. Ferguson 1896)
(King, "Letter from Birmingham Jail," 1963)
Tips
  • Every source cited in your essay must appear in your bibliography.
  • Every source in your bibliography must be cited at least once in your essay.
  • Do not over-quote — use quotation when exact wording matters, otherwise paraphrase and cite.
