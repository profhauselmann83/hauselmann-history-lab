About This Documentary

“Rise! The Road to Civil Rights (1940–1968)”

This is Episode 5 of The African Americans: Many Rivers to Cross, the PBS series hosted by historian Henry Louis Gates, Jr. It covers the full arc of the modern civil rights movement: Black veterans returning from World War II to face Jim Crow and racial violence at home, the Montgomery Bus Boycott, Ruby Bridges desegregating her New Orleans school, the Nashville sit-in campaign, Bloody Sunday at Selma, and the emergence of Black Power after King’s assassination in 1968.

As you watch, keep the week’s central question in mind: The postwar United States promised opportunity and freedom — but for whom? What strategies did excluded groups use to claim what democracy promised them? Pay close attention to the debate between King and Malcolm X, and to what Stokeley Carmichael says about nonviolence at the end — that tension runs directly into your source analysis and discussion this week.
