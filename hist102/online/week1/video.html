Video Lecture

Watch before beginning the readings — approx. 30 minutes

~30 min

This week, we're exploring one of the most important and complicated chapters in American history — the end of Reconstruction. In this lecture, I'll guide you through the competing visions for rebuilding the nation after the Civil War, the rise of new freedoms and new barriers for formerly enslaved people, and why Reconstruction ultimately collapsed in 1877.

As you watch, think about how the questions raised during this period — about freedom, equality, and citizenship — still echo in our society today.

▶️ Watch the full video before moving on to the readings. You'll encounter the figures and debates from this lecture throughout both reading pages.

Questions to Keep in Mind While Watching

These questions will guide your reading and come up again in the Canvas discussion.

  1. What are the major differences between the Radical Republicans and Andrew Johnson's approaches to Reconstruction?
  2. Which plan do you think would be more likely to unite the country after the Civil War — and why?
  3. Why might the Radical Republican plan have been considered radical at the time?
  4. Based on what you know, what do you predict actually happened during Reconstruction?
Heads Up After the video, the readings will introduce you to two key primary sources: a speech by Thaddeus Stevens advocating for land redistribution and civil rights, and speeches by Andrew Johnson opposing federal intervention. You'll analyze those documents in the Canvas discussion at the end of the week.
