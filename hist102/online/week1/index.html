The Era of Reconstruction

1865–1877 — Competing visions for rebuilding the nation after the Civil War

~3.5 hrs reading 60 pts graded

Reconstruction was a time of significant political and social change in the United States. In this module, we will examine the controversial origins of Reconstruction and evaluate its successes and failures. We will explore how formerly enslaved people struggled against prejudice and political repression as they attempted to build new lives — and ask why Reconstruction came to an abrupt end in 1877.

Learning Objectives

  • Identify the objectives of presidential and congressional Reconstruction and assess the impact on white and African-American residents of the South
  • Analyze and interpret primary source documents from the nineteenth century using historical research methods

Central Question for the Week

"Why was the Radical Republican plan for Reconstruction considered ‘radical’?"

You will answer this question in the Canvas discussion after completing both readings.
